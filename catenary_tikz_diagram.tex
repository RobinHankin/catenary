We consider a unit-length hanging chain in the form of a catenary
constrained to pass through $(0,1)$ and $(x,\cosh(x/a))$; $x$ and $a$
to be determined.  The chain is of unit weight per unit length and is
subject to (known) force $F$ pulling horizontally to the left;
figure~\ref{catenary}.

\begin{eqnarray}
  F &=& T_x\qquad\mbox{balance horizontal forces}\\
  T_y &=& a\sinh(x/a)\qquad\mbox{vertical forces; arc length}\\
  T_y/T_x &=& \sinh(x/a)\qquad\mbox{tension parallel to chain}
\end{eqnarray}

We see that $a=F=T_x$.  The arc length is
$\int\sqrt{1+y'^2}\,dx=a\sinh(x/a)=1$, whence $F\sinh(x/F)=1$, or
$x=F\mathrm{arcsinh}{\left(1/F\right)}$.

This is nonlinear for small $F$:

\begin{eqnarray}\frac{dx}{dF}&=&
  \mathrm{arcsinh}{\left(1/F\right)}-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+F^2}}\\
  &=& \log\left(\frac{1}{F}+\sqrt{1+\frac{1}{F^2}}\right)-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+F^2}}\\
  &=& \log\left(\frac{1}{F}\left(1+\sqrt{1+F^2}\right)\right)-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+F^2}}\\
  &=& -\log F + \log\left(1+\sqrt{1+F^2}\right)-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+F^2}}\\
  &=& -\log F + \log 2 -1 - \frac{1}{4}F^2 + \mathcal{O}(F^4)\\
  &\longrightarrow&\infty\qquad\mbox{as $F\longrightarrow 0^+$}
  \end{eqnarray}

The vertical displacement $y$ is given by $F\cosh(x/F)-F$ with
$x=F\mathrm{arcsinh}(1/F)$, or
$y=\sqrt{1+F^2}-F=1-F+\mathcal{O}(F^2)$.

If we want to consider the case where $F$ is very large (resulting in
an almost horizontal string), we obtain a horizontal displacement of
$1-\frac{1}{6}F^{-2} + \mathcal{O}(F^{-4})$ and a vertical
displacement of $\frac{1}{2}F^{-1} + \mathcal{O}(F^{-2})$.
